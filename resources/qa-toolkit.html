QA Translation Dictionary

Regression Testing (what devs say)

Business translation: "Making sure new updates don't break existing features." It's confirming your foundation stays solid as you build new floors.

Edge Cases (what devs say)

Business translation: "Unusual but possible user scenarios." Think of them as rare customer situations that could cause complaints if overlooked.

User Acceptance Testing (what devs say)

Business translation: "Does it actually solve the problem for real users?" It's test-driving your product with actual users before full launch.

Test Coverage (what devs say)

Business translation: "How much of your app has been tested?" It's like an insurance policy - higher percentage means lower risk.

Test Environment (what devs say)

Business translation: "A safe practice area." It's like a restaurant's test kitchen where chefs experiment before updating the menu.

Production (what devs say)

Business translation: "The live, customer-facing version of your product." Think of it as your store during business hours - changes affect real customers.

Simplified QA Flowchart for Founders

Define Success

What does "working" actually mean for this feature?

Verify Core Flow

Does the main user journey work as expected?

Test Boundaries

What happens when users do unexpected things?

Real User Check

Watch actual users interact with the feature

Non-Technical Founder's QA Checklist

  • Always ask: "How do we know this is working?" for each new feature
  • Document what "working" means before development starts
  • Test your product on the oldest devices your users might have
  • Try using your product with a poor internet connection
  • Ask a non-team member to use your product without instructions
  • Focus testing time on features that directly affect revenue
